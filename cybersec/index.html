About Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices. It includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can have serious, lasting effects.

Signs of Cyberbullying

  • Unexpected changes in mood or behavior
  • Avoiding social interactions or school
  • Unexpected changes in device usage patterns
  • Secrecy about online activities

Prevention Strategies

Preventing cyberbullying before it starts can help foster a more positive online environment. Educating yourself and others about cyberbullying and its effects is crucial.

Educate

Learn about the causes and consequences of cyberbullying to better understand how to prevent it.

Communicate

Open lines of communication with family and friends about online experiences and witnessed behaviors.

Protect

Use privacy settings and reporting tools to protect yourself and others online. Be Cautious anytime.

Resources and Support

It's crucial to report cyberbullying incidents to appropriate authorities and seek help from trusted sources. Here are some resources that can provide guidance and support:

  • ReachOut Australia: Check out au.reachout.com for articles and support on dealing with cyberbullying in Australia.
  • eSafety Commissioner: Visit esafety.gov.au for guidance and reporting tools specifically tailored to combat cyberbullying in Australia.
  • Youth Beyond Blue: Explore youthbeyondblue.com for resources and strategies to cope with cyberbullying, with a focus on Australian youth.
  • Healthdirect Australia: Visit healthdirect.gov.au for information on cyberbullying and steps to address it.
  • Cyberbullying Research Center: Access research-based resources and strategies for dealing with cyberbullying at cyberbullying.org.
  • Local Authorities: Contact law enforcement or local support services for immediate assistance and advice.
